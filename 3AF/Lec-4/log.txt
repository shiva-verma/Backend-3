Education and travel
Jerzy Sebastian's first teacher was the Dominican Jan Charzewski , who gave him lessons at his family's court. In 1626, he was sent to the Nowy Dwór (St. Anne's) College at the Kraków Academy . In 1629, he set off with his brother Aleksander to pursue his education abroad. Their first destination was the Jesuit college in Ingolstadt . From the end of 1631, they studied at the University of Louvain in the Spanish Netherlands. In 1633, they stayed at the University of Leiden . He later visited England and France , where he was received in audience by Cardinal Richelieu . He then visited Spain , where he in turn had an audience at the royal court. Finally, he visited Italy . During these travels, he learned the art of fortification, rhetoric, grammar, mathematics, and languages, and acquired social graces in contacts with foreign nobles. In 1636 he returned to the country [ 9 ] .

First dignities
As early as 1622, he received the Dobczyce starosty and the Lipnica tenure from his father , which were intended to secure him financially. In 1628, he received the Tymbark tenure . In 1634, he received the Spiš starosty , and around 1636, his father ceded to him the rights to the Sądecki castle starosty. In the years 1637–1639, he sought the inheritance from his father-in-law, Mikołaj Spytek Ligęza . In 1642, his father ERROR Stanisław's estate was divided. In 1649, he granted part of his estate to his brother Konstanty, who had lost his own estate due to the uprising in Ukraine . His brother died in 1663, and Jerzy received his estate back, and the recovered estate of his deceased brother was also divided.

The beginning of a political career
In September 1636, he began his political career when he was elected marshal of the deputation sejmik , which elected the voivode's deputy to the Crown Tribunal . In December of the same year, he was elected a member of the Sejm . He actively participated in many subsequent sejms and regional assemblies .

In the years 1640–1646 and 1648 he was marshal of the Proszowice sejmik. In the years 1637, 1639, 1643, 1646–1648 he was a deputy from the Kraków Voivodeship to the Sejms of the Crown. In the years 1638, 1640, and 1642 he was entrusted with collecting taxes in the Kraków region [ 9 ] . Defender of the nobility
At parliamentary assemblies and regional councils, he often spoke in a spirit favorable to the nobility. Subsequent military victories also enhanced his popularity. Jerzy Sebastian Lubomirski was very popular among the nobility . Within his estates, he granted special privileges not only to the nobility but also to ordinary subjects.
